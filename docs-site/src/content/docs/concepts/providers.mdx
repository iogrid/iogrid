iogrid is a two-sided market. This page is for people considering becoming a
provider, and for people who want to understand how the supply side works.

## The four payout tiers

When a provider installs iogrid, they pick how they want to be paid. They can
switch tiers any time. Tier choice affects future earnings only — never
retroactive.

### Tier 1 — Cash

- **$0.30 per GB of bandwidth shared** (industry standard $0.30–0.60).
- Typical home device sharing 30 GB/month = **~$9 per month**.
- Stripe Connect payout, $10 minimum threshold.
- 1099 issued at year-end for >$600 (US tax compliance).
- **Audience:** people who actively google "passive income at home".
- **Our cost: $9/month per provider. Margin: 0%.**

### Tier 2 — Free VPN (the mesh-swap advantage)

- Bandwidth share pays for **unlimited iogrid VPN** on all the provider's
  devices (iOS, Android, Mac, Windows, Linux).
- Marginal cost to iogrid: ~$0.20/month per provider.
- Perceived value: equivalent to NordVPN's $5/month plan.
- **Audience:** tech-savvy users already paying $60–130/yr for a VPN.
- **Our cost: $0.20/month. Margin: ~98%.**

This is the tier no proxy incumbent can match. Honeygain has no VPN product.
Bright Data has Hola, which is the thing iogrid is the anti-version of.

### Tier 3 — $GRID token

- Solana SPL token, **deflationary**, paid weekly.
- Auto-locked the moment it's distributed — see
  [tokenomics](/concepts/tokenomics/) for the full vesting and burn mechanics.
- Optional opt-in lockup tiers: Loyalty (1.25×), Conviction (1.5×), Maximum
  (2.0×) earnings multipliers in exchange for longer cliffs.
- **Audience:** long-term believers who want exposure to network growth.

### Tier 4 — Charity / mission

- Cash equivalent donated to a chosen cause: EFF, Tor Project, Wikipedia,
  Doctors Without Borders, etc.
- Provider receives a tax receipt from the recipient.
- **Audience:** ideologically motivated providers. Small but PR-strong.
- **Our cost: $9/month per provider (donated). Margin: 0%, brand value high.**

## Realistic monthly earnings

Conservative numbers assuming a provider opts into **all enabled workloads**:

| Device profile | Bandwidth | Docker | GPU | iOS-build | Total/mo |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Home desktop, Linux, no GPU | $9 | $16 | — | — | **$25** |
| Home desktop, Linux, RTX 4070 | $9 | $16 | $55 | — | **$80** |
| Apple Silicon Mac Mini, idle 4h/day | $9 | $8 | $4 | $145 | **$166** |
| Idle 24/7 datacenter server | $30 | $80 | $90 | — | **$200** |

The Mac Mini is the breakout: at $0.04/Xcode-minute, four hours of CI-quality
build time per day at half-capacity utilization yields ~$145/month with the
host computer 99% idle the rest of the time.

## Scheduling fairness

The scheduler optimizes for: customer SLA · provider equity · cost.

- **Round-robin within capability/geo buckets.** Two providers with identical
  capabilities in the same country see ~50/50 long-run distribution.
- **Quality score nudges.** Uptime, p95 latency, success rate, and customer
  ratings produce a multiplier between 0.5× and 1.5×. Bad providers get less
  load until they recover; great providers get more.
- **No "pay to play".** Providers cannot bid for more work. The only knob is
  capability + caps + opt-in categories.

## Provider opt-ins (categorical consent)

A core piece of iogrid's anti-Hola posture: providers don't just agree to
"share bandwidth", they consent to **categories** of traffic. Default opt-in
set:

- ✅ E-commerce monitoring
- ✅ SEO / SERP scraping
- ✅ Ad verification
- ✅ Travel aggregation
- ✅ Lead-gen scraping
- ✅ AI training data
- 🟡 Social media intelligence — opt-in required
- 🟡 Adult content destinations — opt-in required
- ❌ Banking domains — opt-in required AND customer KYC
- ❌ Government / military (.gov, .mil) — blocked unconditionally
- ❌ CSAM hash matches — blocked unconditionally
- ❌ SMTP / IRC / Tor exit ports — blocked at the protocol level

Providers can also blocklist individual destinations (regex) and revoke
category opt-ins instantly.

## Daemon lifecycle

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Install" icon="rocket">
    One-line `curl | bash`. Daemon installs as a system service (`launchd`,
    `systemd`, or Windows service). Open source installer.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Link" icon="approve-check-circle">
    Local control UI shows a six-digit code. Paste it at
    [link.iogrid.org](https://link.iogrid.org). mTLS keys provisioned via
    Coordinator's CA.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Heartbeat" icon="seti:lock">
    15-second heartbeats. Long-lived gRPC stream. Daemon dials out — never
    accepts inbound public connections.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Drain" icon="seti:powershell">
    Provider clicks **Drain** or runs `iogridctl drain`. Coordinator stops
    routing new workloads; in-flight workloads finish.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Uninstall" icon="error">
    `iogridctl uninstall`. Cleans up all installed files, certificates,
    cgroups, WireGuard interfaces. Audit log preserved on coordinator for
    90 days.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>
