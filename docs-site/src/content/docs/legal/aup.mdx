## You may use iogrid to

- Run public-web scraping at scale (e-commerce monitoring, SEO, price
  aggregation, public-business contact discovery, AI training data on public
  pages)
- Verify ad placements and brand-safety crawls
- Run Docker batch jobs, ML inference, GPU rendering
- Build iOS apps in CI / CD pipelines
- Aggregate flights, hotels, car rentals from public booking sites

## You may **not** use iogrid to

- Distribute, store, or transit CSAM. **Any hash match is automatically
  blocked and may result in mandatory law-enforcement reporting.**
- Conduct DDoS attacks, "stresser" services, or any other deliberate
  service-degradation attack
- Send unsolicited bulk email (we block SMTP outbound at the protocol level,
  so this also fails technically)
- Coordinate harassment campaigns or doxxing
- Engage in unauthorized access to systems (no penetration testing without
  the target's written consent)
- Launder counterfeit credentials, stolen credit card data, or other
  illegal commodities
- Run cryptocurrency miners — they violate every provider's good-faith
  expectation of what "share idle compute" means
- Scrape data that you're contractually prohibited from scraping under
  another agreement (we don't enforce arbitrary website ToS, but if a
  destination has a court-issued injunction against you, that's your problem)

## Public-data scraping legality (US perspective)

iogrid's posture follows the *hiQ Labs v. LinkedIn* line of cases (Ninth
Circuit, 2017–2022, eventually settled): scraping publicly available data
does not by itself violate the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA).

This is **not legal advice**. Laws differ by jurisdiction and by what
exactly you're scraping. iogrid customers are responsible for their own
compliance.

## Customer responsibilities

You are responsible for:

- **What you target.** You name the destination, you take the risk.
- **What you collect.** Personal data scraping has GDPR / CCPA / state-law
  implications.
- **How you use it.** Bias-laundering, illegal redistribution, etc. are on you.
- **What you sign with us.** When you accept these terms, you indemnify
  iogrid against claims arising from your use of the platform.

## iogrid responsibilities

We commit to:

- Running aggressive pre-flight anti-abuse filters (see
  [anti-abuse](/security/anti-abuse/))
- Cooperating with law enforcement on **specific** lawful requests with
  proper process (not bulk fishing expeditions)
- Notifying customers of LE requests targeting their accounts (unless gagged)
- Maintaining the [defense fund](/security/anti-abuse/#provider-protections)
  for providers acting in good faith

## Penalties for AUP violations

| Severity | First offense | Repeat |
|---|---|---|
| Configuration / accidental | Warning + remediation | Throttle / temporary suspension |
| Negligent | Suspension pending review | Account termination |
| Wilful / criminal | Immediate termination + LE cooperation | n/a |

Termination triggers a 7-day data-export window before deletion. CSAM-hash
matches trigger immediate termination with no export window.

## Appealing

If your account is suspended and you believe in error, email
[appeals@iogrid.org](mailto:appeals@iogrid.org) with your account ID and a
description. Appeals are reviewed by a person, not an automated system, with
a target turnaround of 5 business days.
